Angalabiri {% trans 'History' %}

This is the birth and journey of Angalabiri people in Sagbama Local Government Area of Bayelsa State, Nigeria.

Settlement on the "Shores of River Forcados"

Before 1815
01. -

Angalabiri Federated Community was founded over 200 years ago before 1815 AD

It was founded by AKOU the son of Angalabiriowei and the grandson of Tarakiriowei.

AKOU was a hunter/fisherman who migrated from Ubioma in present Southern-Ijaw LGA of Bayelsa State. He first settled at Uwubulou (opposite present Amatolo) after a misunderstanding amongst Tarakiriowei sons over food. The Angalabiri kindred also settled at the present location of Amatolo before moving to Edeinpou on the shores of River Forcados estuary (near present Delta State Angiama).

Thereafter, the Angalabiri kindred negotiated for its present location with Zado, son of Mein and the founder of Oboro Community in present Delta State. Fortunately, Zado was a lone settler far away from his Mein kindred so he was willing to leave the land and return to his father’s domain i.e. the present location of Oboro. Thus, Zado sold the land to the Angalabiri kindred through trade-by-barter. Initially, Angalabiri Federated Community settled with four quarters viz: Ozigbopelei, Aramabiri, Olodiamapelei and Agbeinware. It later expanded to five quarters with the creation of Guaware. The five quarters further developed to federating units as communities viz; Ozibgo-ama, Aran-ama, Olodiama, Agbein-ama and Gua-ama, making the modern Angalabiri Federated Community with an indigenous population of over ten thousand people.
Earlier, there was an unbridled culture of idol-worshipping amongst the sons of AKOU Angalabiriowei, as the discovery of the Eperetua Deity by AKOU, the founder of Angalabiri Federated Community was the precursor to the discovery of the land, Eperetuabou (Epiritua Bush) and the attendant final relocation/settlement of the community at its present location. History has it that earlier Chief Priests of Eperetua were buried at Owubulou (opposite Amatolo) according to customs\tradition. It was said that Saisai, Enewarikemieye, etc were among Chief Priests of Eperetua that were buried at Owubulou. The first Chief Priest that was buried at the present location of Angalabiri was Kuro-Orulagha Ebakpa.

Angalabiri Traditional Stool

1937-2016
02. -

Angalabiri has a traditional stool of Amananaowei (Paramount Ruler) recognized by Bayelsa State Government and documented in the Gazette as a Second Class Stool

The Amananaowei of Angalabiri is greeted as: “AKOU!, AKOU!!, AKOU!!!, Datimi Ondu (Long live the Paramount Ruler)”. The Angalabiri traditional stool started with Boubaikeburu Mieyeseigha as the first Amananaowei that was traditionally recognized. He reigned from 1937 till his death in 1971. Thereafter, the stool was vacant till 2004, when Alex Youbogha became the Amananaowei. He was the first to be recognized and documented in the Gazette of Bayelsa State Government as AKOU I HRH Alex Youbogha also joined his ancestors in 2016, which made the Amananaowei stool vacant, again. Constitutional Laws, Customs and Traditional Values

Before 1815 - Date
03. -

Angalabiri Federated Community operates a written Constitution that was first enacted in 1989

Prior to 1989, the Community was operating an unwritten pre-colonial Constitution based on historical facts and its traditional values. The Angalabiri constitutional development has witnessed four major constitutional amendments viz: 1989 (amendment from unwritten to written constitution), 2004 (amendment of the 1989 Constitution), and 2015 (amendment of the 2004 Constitution). It was the 2015 amendment that recognized five quarters as federating units (communities) and eventually declared Angalabiri a Federated Community (See S.2 of the Constitution as Amended). The 2015 Constitution as Amended also reviewed the tenure of Amananaowei and the Title Chiefs from three years to life.

Angalabiri Federated Community also has a recognized Community Development Committee (CDC), headed by a Chairman and EXCO Members. The Constitution allows one-year tenure for the CDC. The 2015 Constitution as Amended further provides Statutory Development Committees on the following areas: (i) Education, (ii) Health, (iii) Festival, (iv) Funeral, (v) Electric Power/Water Supply (Utility), (vi) Town Planning, and (vii) Market. The Constitution allows two-year tenure for all Statutory Development Committees.

The 2019 constitution (amendment of 2015 constitution) has provided specific requirements for the removal of the CDC Chairman and Members. This has laid to rest the babaric approach of any aggrieved group proclaiming the community with a GONG announcing the dethronement of a CDC Chairman at the slightest provocation.

The Angalabiri people has two renowned annual festivals viz: the Aburubele Oge whereby epic masquerades like Eperitua, Fiyaifikakuro and others featured prominently; and the Abobulu (Island) Fishing Festival which is predominantly celebrated in April every year. Angalabiri Federated Community is also blessed with music (a.k.a Congo City). It has produced an amazing number of Ijaw musicians such as the Late Legend, the Numoupere of Ijaw, King (Dr) Robert Ebizimor, Late Bestman Doupere, SAN A.S. Eseduwo, Anthony Cockson, Peanock Fukurumo, DKC Lagos, Newman Zink, Late Fine Dieware, Princess Diana, Kente Bekeyei, etc. Angalabiri Federated Community has enjoyed peaceful co-existence with its neighbouring communities over the years, e.g. Ofoni, Adobu, Toru-Orua, Bulou-Orua, Ebedebiri, Angiama, etc.

The late legend King(Dr) Robert Ebizimor is celebrated every year on 31st July whereby all the IZON musicians gather for live performance by way of reaffirming their talents. This is also another canival in angalabiri federated community that attracts tourists from all over the world.

Our Natural Resources

Before 1815-Date
04. -

The Community has a beautiful scenic view

The community is located by the River Forcados, overlooking the age-long ABOBULU island, which serves tripple purpose of fishing, farming and eco-green environment.

The River Forcados can also serve as a source for Hydro Energy generation.

With fishing lakes like Abobulu, Dunoubou, Kalaboye, Tamowei, Ogbalabeni, Etoindunou, Bienmadanwain, Torukubu, etc, our community engages majorly in the business of fishing.

The community is also blessed with other natural resources, such as gravel, sharp sand, timber, etc. This largely explains the main occupations of the people of Angalabiri i.e farming, fishing, hunting, local dredging, lumbering, and so on.

Present
06. -
